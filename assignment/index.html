Identifying Usability Problems

In this assignment, students were tasked with identifying a website with Usability issues, visually redesigning the website, and finally building the redesign using HTML and CSS. To start, I chose irishwrecksonline.net a website that provides information on dive-able shipwrecks around Ireland. The website has a lot of usability issues, including a lack of alt text, poor color contrast, and a lack of mobile responsiveness. The design also dated and there are a lot of creative ways that this information can be presented. Second, the website's design is simple but unintuitive so I believe I can significantly improve the user experience. Finally, I am interested in shipwrecks and diving and enjoy the content of the website. The website is pictured below.

Finding Problems

Below are all usability problems found in the website

  • At first glance, your eyes are directed to the map on the right side, but it’s unclear what you’re looking at. The title of the website is large, but doesn’t tell you exactly what the website does.
  • There are a lot of colors on the webpage, making it hard to read certain text and understand what’s important
  • When hovering your mouse over buttons and clickable objects, they don’t change colors making it challenging to understand what’s clickable or not.
  • The efficiency of the website is lackluster because you may have to click through many links to get the information you’re looking for. The search bar is also challenging to find and use.
  • Since all the buttons look the same, it may be challenging for users to remember which button they pressed to access the same information that they need.
  • The learnability is moderate. The website’s main page doesn’t have an overwhelming amount of information; however, it’s unclear what a user is able to do from the homepage.
  • The map element is apparently clickable and directs to another page with a more interactive map. This was very unclear when using the website initially.
  • The axis on the map image are also confusing and have no relation to the photo.
  • There is no mobile responsiveness, making it difficult to use on a phone or tablet.
  • There is no alt text on the images, making it difficult for visually impaired users to understand the content.

The WAVE Report mentioned that the website is missing alt text on a lot of its images, and it’s also missing documentation of the language that’s used. The website is also missing a heading structure and a label. For one, the lack of alt text makes it difficult for visually impaired readers to understand what the site content has. In addition, the page has no heading structure or page regions. This makes it harder for a search engine or LLM to obtain information from the website which makes the website harder to reach and thus inaccessible.
